Dyson did not invent the cordless stick vacuum, but it **defined** what people expect: strong suction, a premium feel, and prices that make you pause before checkout. If you are shopping from outside the U.S. or comparing imports, this guide is about **fit**, not hype—so you do not overpay for features you will never use.

## Who a Dyson actually suits

- **Apartments and small homes** with mixed hard floor and rugs
- **Pet owners** who need reliable pickup on short-pile carpet
- **People who hate dragging a cord** but still want “real” suction on demand
- **Buyers who value design and resale** more than the cheapest spec sheet

It is a weaker default if you need **90+ minutes** of nonstop cleaning on one charge, have mostly thick carpet, or want a sub-$200 machine and do not care about brand.

## What to compare (in plain language)

| Factor | Why it matters |
|--------|----------------|
| **Suction / motor** | Higher tiers (V12 Detect, V15 Detect, Gen5detect) lift more from rugs; older V8-class models are fine for hard floor and light mess |
| **Runtime** | Eco modes stretch minutes; max mode drains fast—check **real-world** reviews, not brochure peaks |
| **Head / laser / anti-tangle** | Laser dust detection helps on hard floor; anti-tangle brush bars matter if you have long hair or pets |
| **Weight & wrist fatigue** | Top models are heavier; a “lighter” line can feel better for stairs and daily quick cleans |
| **Filters & cost of ownership** | HEPA-style filtration is great for allergies; replacement filters and batteries are part of the long-term bill |
| **Warranty & voltage** | Overseas buyers: confirm **local warranty**, plug type, and whether the bundle includes the right charger |

::: tip
Buy for the **floor types you have today**, not the home you might move into in three years. Most regret comes from paying for Detect/laser tiers on mostly carpet.
:::

## Model tiers (quick map)

**Entry / value cordless** — V8-class lines still sell well: enough suction for hard floor, crumbs, and light rugs; shorter runtime than new flagships. Good if Dyson is a “nice to have,” not a lifetime appliance.

**Mid / daily driver** — V10 or V11 (where still available new) sit in the middle: better runtime and tooling than V8, without every 2026 flagship feature.

**Current sweet spot for many homes** — **V12 Detect Slim** balances weight, laser illumination on hard floor, and strong suction. A common pick for urban apartments and mixed surfaces.

**Flagship / max performance** — **V15 Detect** and **Gen5detect** push suction and intelligence (particle sensing, auto power). Worth it for larger homes, pets, and buyers who want the best Dyson will sell—not for occasional quick sweeps.

**Wet + dry / wash formats** — Lines like **Dyson WashG1** or combo systems target different jobs (hard-floor washing). Treat them as a **second device**, not a drop-in replacement for a stick vacuum unless that is explicitly what you need.

## What we usually skip

- **Paying flagship money for a spare room** you vacuum twice a month
- **Import grey-market units** with no local warranty or mismatched voltage
- **Ignoring the brush head**—the wrong head wastes suction on carpet or scratches delicate floor
- **Chasing “Detect”** if you never vacuum in low light and already see dust on hard floor

## Before you click buy

1. Open the product page and match **exact model number** (V15 is not one SKU—bundles differ).
