Most U.S. kitchen cans take **13-gallon drawstring bags**. The real decision is not brand loyalty— it is **fit on your rim, cost per bag, and whether the bag survives wet kitchen waste** without tearing on the way to the dumpster. A **~$12** 80-count box and a **~$20** 110-count box can look similar until you divide by count and add **Subscribe & Save**. If you shop on Amazon from outside the U.S. or are stocking a rental alongside other [home basics](/en/posts/robot-vacuum-under-500-2026/), this guide is about **fit**, not box art.

## Who 13-gallon kitchen bags actually suit

- **Standard tall kitchen trash cans** (13 gal / ~49 L) with a drawstring closure
- **Households that change the kitchen bag every 1— days** and want fewer mid-carry blowouts
- **Buyers who compare total cost**— count per box matters as much as shelf price
- **Amazon Subscribe & Save shoppers** who can lock in recurring delivery on a stable ASIN

It is a weaker default if your can is **10 gal or smaller**, you only need **dry office or bathroom liners**, or you prefer **unscented only** and the listing you find is scented— always read the variant line.

## What to compare (in plain language)

| Factor | Why it matters |
|--------|----------------|
| **13-gallon fit** | Too short sags inside the can; too wide fights the rim. Match your can label |
| **Drawstring strength** | Kitchen bags get lifted full and wet— weak ties snap at the worst moment |
| **Thickness / "Ultra Strong" claims** | Wet food scraps and sharp packaging need more puncture resistance |
| **Cost per bag** | Divide box price by count; a cheaper box with half the bags often loses |
| **Kitchen vs dry waste** | Kitchen bags need more strength; dry recycling bins can use lighter liners |
| **Scent** | Scented masks odor short-term; unscented is better near food prep for many people |
| **Subscribe & Save** | 5–5% off plus delivery rhythm— only worth it if count and size stay stable |

::: tip
Calculate **cost per bag before you buy**. A $20 box is not automatically expensive if it includes 110 bags (~$0.18 each) vs an $12 box with 50 bags (~$0.24 each).
:::

## Kitchen bag vs lighter liner — quick call

| Use a **13 gal kitchen drawstring** if:| Use a **lighter liner or smaller size** if:|
|----------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| Bag stays in the **tall kitchen can** daily | Bin is **bathroom, office, or dry recycling only** |
| You toss **food scraps, coffee filters, wet items** | Waste is **paper and plastic with no food liquid** |
| You **lift the full bag out** by the drawstring | Can is **10 gal or smaller** —13 gal sags and wastes film |
| You want **one SKU on Subscribe & Save** | You split **kitchen vs dry waste** and optimize each bin |

Pairing a strong kitchen bag with a separate dry bin (no liner or thin liner) often cuts total bag spend without inviting leaks.

## What we usually skip

- **Buying on box price alone** without dividing by bag count
- **13-gallon labels on cans that are actually 10 gal** — fit suffers and you overpay for unused plastic
- **Scented bags** when cooking smells already bother you— or unscented when you wanted odor masking
- **Subscribe & Save on a listing** whose count or size keeps changing between restocks
- **Using thin budget film** for dripping food waste and blaming the drawstring when the bottom splits

## Before you click buy

1. Match your can sticker: **13 gal / 49 L** tall kitchen, not office 7–0 gal sizes.
2. On the product page, confirm **exact count** (80 vs 110 vs multi-roll total) and **scented vs unscented**.
3. Divide **price ÷ count** and compare to your current box— Subscribe & Save discount applies after that math.
4. Read **recent** 1-star reviews for **wrong size shipped**, thin film, or drawstrings that pull out of the bag.
5. Prices and Subscribe & Save tiers change on Amazon— confirm the **current price, seller, and delivery option** before checkout.

Run the per-bag math, split kitchen vs dry bins where you can, and use Subscribe & Save only on a stable SKU you actually go through.
