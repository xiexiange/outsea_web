In many markets the **HD17** name maps to the **Dyson Supersonic r™** hair dryer: a smaller, lighter take on the classic Supersonic™ built for fast drying and more precise home styling. If you shop cross-border or compare Amazon regions, this guide is about **fit for your hair and routine**, not salon marketing.

## Who HD17 / Supersonic r™ actually suits

- **Medium-to-long hair** you blow-dry several times a week and want less wrist fatigue
- **Fine or frizz-prone hair** where ionic airflow and a proper cold shot matter
- People who will actually use **multiple magnetic attachments** (quick dry, gentle, smoothing, concentrator)
- Buyers who value **heat control, motor tech, and resale** more than the cheapest wattage on paper

It is a weaker default if you only want the hottest, fastest blast on a tight budget, or if you really need a **multi-styler** (look at Airwrap—not HD17).

## What to compare (in plain language)

| Factor | Why it matters |
|--------|----------------|
| **Name / SKU** | **HD17 ≈ Supersonic r™**; **HD16** is usually Supersonic Nural™; the round-barrel line is classic Supersonic™ |
| **Weight** | r™ is marketed ~**30% smaller, 20% lighter** than classic Supersonic host; ~325g class body weight in many bundles |
| **Power & voltage** | Often **1700W, 230V** variants—confirm plug, voltage, and warranty for your country |
| **RFID attachments** | Five intelligent nozzles store last-used heat/airflow; r™ uses its own attachment family |
| **Heat control** | Intelligent heat regulation reduces extreme heat exposure—still use lower heat near ends |
| **Filter care** | Handle filter needs cleaning; a flashing LED warns when airflow is restricted |

::: tip
Decide whether you need a **light precision dryer** (Supersonic r™ / HD17) or a **curling multi-styler** (Airwrap). They solve different jobs.
:::

## Model map (quick)

**Main pick: HD17 / Supersonic r™** — Streamlined heater, Hyperdymium™ motor (up to ~110,000rpm in marketing specs), five smart attachments, built for daily dry + light styling.

**Scalp-focused: HD16 / Supersonic Nural™** — Scalp protect modes and sensors; worth a look if you dry close to the roots on sensitive skin.

**Value / ecosystem: classic Supersonic™** — Mature accessory market, sometimes lower street price; larger body but still a strong dryer.

**Different category: Airwrap** — Curling and shaping system—not a drop-in substitute for a dedicated dryer.

## What we usually skip

- **Flagship money** for hair you only air-dry or blast for two minutes
- **Grey-market units** with wrong voltage or no local warranty
- Never cleaning the filter, then blaming weak airflow
- Buying HD17 when you mainly need **curling barrels** (Airwrap territory)

## Before you click buy

1. Match the **exact model and bundle** (Straight+Wavy vs Curly+Coily kits differ).
2. Verify **voltage, plug type, and warranty region**—HD17 is often engineered for the purchase country’s voltage.

4. Compare **total price** with HD16 Nural, classic Supersonic, or strong mid-tier brands (Shark, Panasonic, etc.).
5. Amazon prices and stock change—confirm **model and attachments** on the product page before checkout.
